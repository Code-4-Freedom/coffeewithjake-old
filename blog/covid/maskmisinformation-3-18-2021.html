There have been two sides of the debate on whether or not Americans need to wear masks in order to protect themselves from COVID-19.

As a science student, I am going to be analyzing the effectiveness of masks from a scientific perspective as oppossed to a political perspective.

According to the Centers for Disease Control (CDC), masks cannot protect individuals from smoke particles.

Thus, if a mask is of little use with smoke, then it is of little use when protecting against COVID-19.

COVID-19 particles can range from 50 nanometers (nm) to 500nm (Depends on study and source). Meanwhile, smoke particles can range from 400nm to 700nm. Industrial masks can only filter out particles greater than 300nm and a 16 layer cloth mask can filter out 300nm particles. (depends on thread count).

Some studies have argured that COVID-19 with water molecules will not go through a mask is true, but not all air and other particles that we breath in or out are mixed with water, and that includes COVID-19.

Thus, masks are not effective in protecting against COVID-19 unless it is being transported via sneeze or other fluid.

OPINION

If I were writing the COVID-19 guidelines I would say,

MASKS: Masks do not definately protect against COVID-19 and is a personal or business choice to utilize them. However, keeping a mask or other cloth item close by for covering sneezes is advised.

HAND WASHING: It is advised that individuals wash their hands regularly for at least 20-30 seconds while using anti-bacterial soap.

SOCIAL INTERACTION: Whenever you leave a place that is not your home, wash or sanitize your hands as this can reduce the number of diseases and viruses carried place to place.

AVOID PUBLIC HARDWARE: Hardware likee hand rails, pens, bathroom door handles, door handles, etc, can hold diseases and viruses for hours or even days. It is advised that you limit the touch of these surfaces and disinfect your hands when you are unable to avoid these surfaces.
