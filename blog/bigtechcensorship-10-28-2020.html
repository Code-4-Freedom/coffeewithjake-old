Big Tech Censorship 10/28/2020

  • Home

Written by Jake on 10/28/2020.

Jack Dorsey defends Twitter censoring the New York Post's article about Hunter Biden.

In the senate hearing against Twitter and Facebook, Jack Dorsey defended censorship, and Sen. Ted Cruz (R) did not approve. Cruz proceeded to ask, "Mr. Dorsey, who the hell elected you and put you in charge of what the media are allowed to report and what the American people are allowed to hear, and why do you persist in behaving as a Democratic super PAC silencing views to the contrary of your political beliefs?"

OPINION:

Cruz had a good point. Twitter is acting like they are in charge of what people can see or read. The problem with this is that Twitter is limiting or banning opposing opinions; thus, the corporation suppressing free speech and press and effectively interfering with the 2020 election.

When Sen. MikeLee (R) asked if the CEOs of Twitter or Facebook could name a leftist/liberal person or entity that their companies had censored, they could not. Dorsey said that there were two congresspeople they had censored, but could not name them.

OPINION:

The CEOs are unable to name any leftist/liberal person or groups that they have censored because they rarely do it. Meanwhile, they are censoring conservatives daily and even censoring bipartisan news sources for posting articles that could hurt Joe Biden's campaign.
